About Me

In 2024, I completed my PhD in the Theoretical Computer Science group at Freie Universität Berlin, supervised by Günter Rote. During my doctoral studies, I was a member of the DFG-funded graduate school Facets of Complexity, through which I also received funding. Additionally, I was a Phase II student at the Berlin Mathematical School (BMS). Before my PhD, I earned my Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Mathematics from the University of Konstanz.

My research interests lie in combinatorial structures and graph drawings. I am particularly fascinated by signotopes - a combinatorial structure which can be visualized by special pseudohyperplane arrangements. Beyond pseudohyperplane arrangements, there are several ways to visualize signotopes. Nice 2- and 3-dimensional visualization can be found here.

Currently, I am working as a software developer at Tablescope, focusing on hybrid game development. Particularly, I develop apps for android and iOS using C# and the Unity game engine.
